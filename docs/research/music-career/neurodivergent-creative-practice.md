# Neurodivergent Creative Practice Research

## Purpose

Research notes for Prism Music/Career on AuDHD-friendly content, self-promotion, publishing confidence, and inconsistent energy.

## Core findings

Neurodivergent creators often need flexible, scaffolded systems instead of daily discipline.

- Batch workflows are more realistic than daily cadence.
- External scaffolding helps: body doubling, calendar prompts, templates, recurring capture windows, and review checklists.
- Templates reduce blank-page paralysis when they provide structure without forcing fake voice.
- Good-enough thresholds matter. Perfectionism and shame can block publishing even after the work is made.
- Reuse and reposting are valid, especially when a creator has years of existing material.
- Inauthentic self-promotion can cause real distress for ND creators, not just weak marketing output.
- Interest/energy fluctuates; a sustainable system should allow catch-up-free restarts.

## Practical implications for Dave

Dave's resistance to fake self-promotion should be treated as a design constraint, not a mindset flaw.

Music/Career should prefer:

- `show the work` over `sell yourself`;
- process notes, honest context, and story-led captions over hype copy;
- batching and parking drafts over daily obligation;
- gentle review queues over pushy reminders;
- one-platform or one-output focus when energy is low;
- explicit discard/delay controls.

## Product implications

The app should include ND-aligned states and language.

Good states:

- idea captured;
- draft parked;
- needs gentle review;
- good enough to share;
- not for public use;
- archive only;
- revisit later.

Bad states/language:

- overdue social post;
- failed cadence;
- must post today;
- personal brand score;
- growth penalty;
- automated persona.

## Design cautions

- Do not build a content treadmill.
- Do not over-reward daily streaks.
- Do not make the system shame Dave for quiet periods.
- Do not generate copy that sounds like a generic influencer.
- Do not auto-post to solve avoidance; this would violate both the authenticity requirement and Beam's external-write approval posture.

## Beam synthesis

This research strongly supports review-first AI assistance and an internal Music/Career cockpit. The system should help Dave decide and publish authentically, not publish on his behalf or perform a fake promotional identity.
