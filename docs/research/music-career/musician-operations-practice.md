# Musician Operations Practice Research

## Purpose

Research notes for Prism Music/Career on how solo/independent musicians and small teams manage email, social cadence, EPKs, contacts, and calendar/task operations.

## Core findings

Independent musician operations are usually simple, low-frequency, and context-driven when they are sustainable.

- Email lists often work best as low-frequency, high-value channels: roughly every 6-8 weeks or around real news, not daily/weekly obligation.
- Fan and industry contacts should be separated. Fans receive human updates; press/venues/presenters receive concise professional material.
- Social media growth advice often favors high posting volume, but working musicians face burnout risk. Sustainable cadence is usually lighter and more selective.
- EPKs should be concise, link-based, mobile-friendly, and easy for press/venues to scan.
- EPK must-haves: short bio, best tracks/media, photos, contact, links, hook/context, and relevant press/show material.
- EPK clutter, huge attachments, stale bios, long PDFs, and generic pitches are usually ignored.
- Touring/performance logistics are often managed with familiar tools: phone calendar, Google Calendar/Sheets, reminders, and simple shared itineraries.

## Practical implications for Dave

Dave needs a system that reduces operational load and keeps separate audiences straight:

- fans/supporters;
- venues/presenters;
- press/media;
- collaborators/commissioners;
- project-specific audiences;
- public EPK viewers.

The product should make it easy to send the right output to the right audience without rewriting everything from scratch.

## Product implications

Music/Career should include or eventually support:

- contact segmentation;
- EPK/press output generation;
- email/story draft generation;
- content cadence guardrails;
- gig/release/promo calendar hooks;
- task creation through Focus review;
- public/private field separation;
- safe exports instead of hidden sends.

## Design cautions

- Do not treat social media as the whole career operation.
- Do not default to daily posting/emailing.
- Do not merge fan and industry contact contexts.
- Do not make a static EPK carry internal operational visibility.
- Do not publish outdated or private data into public exports.

## Beam synthesis

The operations research is the strongest reason to subsume EPK into Prism Music/Career. EPK is necessary, but it is only one artifact in a larger operational system.
