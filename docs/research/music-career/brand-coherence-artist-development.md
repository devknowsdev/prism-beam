# Brand and Identity Strategy for Distinct Music Projects

## Purpose

Research notes for Prism Music/Career on how a multi-hyphenate musician can present distinct projects coherently without flattening them into one fake persona.

## Core findings

Musicians often either unify all their styles under one name or deliberately separate them. The practical pattern is usually hybrid rather than ideological.

- Separate monikers can help when projects have genuinely different audiences, sounds, aesthetics, or emotional contracts.
- Umbrella identity can work when there is an underlying personal, sonic, philosophical, or career thread.
- Real-name crediting is normal for commissioned/institutional work such as film, television, theatre, and composition.
- Alias use is more common for personal/pop-adjacent/scene-specific projects and should be decided per project, not as a suite-wide toggle.
- Audience coherence matters, but artists often overestimate how much listeners require one tidy brand. Devoted listeners often follow authenticity and context across genres.
- A public persona should be an honest extension of the artist, not a fabricated marketing role.

## Practical implications for Dave

Dave's live-looping, improvised electronic, acoustic, and commissioned composition work should not be flattened into one generic persona.

A useful Music/Career system should support:

- a real-name umbrella for professional/commissioned credibility;
- project modes or sub-identities for live-looping, improvised electronic, acoustic, and commissioned work;
- mode-specific public outputs such as EPK variants, booking packets, press blurbs, and social copy;
- a shared underlying story/ethos so the work feels coherent without pretending every project is the same.

## Product implications

The system should avoid a single global `brand voice` field that overwrites nuance.

Suggested model:

```text
Artist umbrella
├── Real-name professional identity
├── Live-looping mode
├── Improvised electronic mode
├── Acoustic mode
└── Commissioned composition mode
```

Each mode can have:

- audience/context;
- tone boundaries;
- representative media;
- short bio/blurb variants;
- public/private notes;
- output destinations.

## Design cautions

- Do not assume one page or one handle is always best.
- Do not force separate pages/handles before there is a real audience or workflow need.
- Do not make the app encourage a fake polished persona.
- Do not treat commissioned work as an alias problem by default.
- Do not require Dave to resolve all public identity questions before drafting useful outputs.

## Beam synthesis

This research supports the Music/Career domain direction: EPK should become one output surface within a broader career system that can handle multiple project modes and public contexts.
